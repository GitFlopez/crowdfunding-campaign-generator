# Prompt 4: Social Media Launch Kit

## Purpose
Generate a complete social media campaign kit across 5 platforms — 23 pieces of content — for the full campaign lifecycle: pre-launch, launch day, and final push.

---

## Prompt

```
You are a social media strategist who has run launch campaigns for crowdfunded products that generated 6-figures in 72 hours. Create a complete social media launch kit for the following campaign.

PROJECT BRIEF:
- Product/Project Name: [NAME]
- What it does: [1-2 sentences]
- Campaign URL: [URL — or "TBD" if not live yet]
- Platform: [Kickstarter / Indiegogo]
- Funding Goal: $[AMOUNT]
- Campaign Duration: [X] days
- Launch date: [DATE or "TBD"]
- Hero visual/video description: [what the main image/video shows]
- Top 2 key benefits: [the 2 things that will stop a scroll]
- Tone: [excited / professional / playful / urgent]

Generate the following 23 pieces of content:

---

LINKEDIN (3 posts):

Post 1 — LAUNCH ANNOUNCEMENT (Post on launch day)
Format: Personal story + product reveal. 150-200 words. Paragraph breaks every 2 sentences for mobile reading. End with campaign link and a question to drive comments.
Tone: Founder story, professional but genuine.

Post 2 — BEHIND THE SCENES (Post on Day 4-7)
Format: "Here's what it took to build this" framing. 100-150 words. Shows the work behind the product. No hard sell — let the story do the selling.
Tone: Transparent, credibility-building.

Post 3 — FINAL PUSH (Post 48 hours before close)
Format: Numbers update (backers funded, % of goal) + urgency + direct CTA. 75-100 words. Social proof heavy.
Tone: Grateful + urgent.

---

TWITTER / X (10 posts):

Post 1 — LAUNCH THREAD OPENER (Launch day)
280 characters. Hook-only. No product name in first line. Ends with "Thread 🧵" to signal more.

Posts 2-7 — LAUNCH THREAD CONTINUATION (6 tweets)
Each 280 characters. Cover: the problem, the solution, how it works (1 feature per tweet), the team credential, social proof (pre-signups or press), and the ask (back us + link).

Post 8 — STANDALONE: DAY 3 MOMENTUM UPDATE
Share a milestone. 280 characters. "We're X% funded. Here's what that means →"

Post 9 — STANDALONE: WEEK 2 RE-ENGAGEMENT
For the "dead middle" of the campaign. 280 characters. New angle — a use case story or customer quote.

Post 10 — STANDALONE: FINAL 24 HOURS
Pure urgency. 280 characters. "[X] hours left. [Y] backers. [Z]% funded. After this: retail price. Link in bio."

---

INSTAGRAM (3 caption + hashtag sets):

Caption 1 — LAUNCH POST (Launch day)
150-200 characters for caption (punchy, visual-first). Hook in first line before "more" cutoff. 
Hashtags (separate): 25-30 hashtags — mix of niche (#kickstarter, #productdesign, #[product category]) and broad (#startup, #innovation).

Caption 2 — LIFESTYLE/USE CASE (Day 5-10)
Show the product in use. 100-150 characters. Aspirational. Ends with CTA to link in bio.
Hashtags (separate): 25-30 hashtags, different set from Caption 1.

Caption 3 — FINAL PUSH (Last 48 hours)
Urgency frame. 75-100 characters. "X hours left to back [PRODUCT] at founding backer pricing. Link in bio."
Hashtags (separate): 15-20 hashtags focused on crowdfunding discovery.

---

FACEBOOK (2 posts):

Post 1 — LAUNCH ANNOUNCEMENT (Launch day)
200-250 words. More detail than Instagram — Facebook readers tolerate longer copy. Include campaign link directly in post (not "link in bio"). Frame for sharing: "Tag someone who needs this."

Post 2 — MILESTONE + SOCIAL PROOF (Mid-campaign)
100-150 words. Share a backer count milestone. Include a backer quote or reaction if available. CTA: "Back us before [DATE] for early pricing."

---

REDDIT (1 post):

Post — COMMUNITY-APPROPRIATE ANNOUNCEMENT
Identify the 3 most relevant subreddits for this product (based on product category). Write a single post that works for all 3 with minor customization.
Format: 200-300 words. Community-first framing (show you're a member of the community, not just promoting). Lead with the problem, not the product. Mention Kickstarter link at the end only. 
NO "BUY MY THING" energy — Reddit communities downvote promotional posts immediately. Write it so it reads like a member sharing something they built.
Include: Suggested subreddits + customization note for each one.

---

After all 23 pieces, add:
POSTING SCHEDULE (table):
- What to post on which day of the campaign
- Platform × day grid showing which content goes live when
- Note: LinkedIn and Reddit tend to drive highest referral traffic for niche products; Twitter/X drives virality; Instagram drives discovery
```

---

## Tips for Best Results

- Save the campaign URL and fill it in after you launch — run this prompt before launch and add the link after.
- For Reddit: Read each subreddit's rules before posting. Some ban self-promotion entirely; others allow it on specific days.
- Schedule LinkedIn and Twitter/X posts using Buffer or Hootsuite so you're not scrambling on launch day.
- Instagram: Use a link-in-bio tool (Linktree, Later) to point to your campaign page.
- The Twitter launch thread is your most repurposable content — it becomes the LinkedIn post, the Reddit post, and the Facebook post with minor editing.
