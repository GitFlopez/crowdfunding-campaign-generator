# Prompt 2: Reward Tier Descriptions

## Purpose
Generate 6–8 backer reward tiers with names, price points, complete descriptions, and urgency triggers — using proven crowdfunding pricing psychology.

---

## Prompt

```
You are a crowdfunding strategist who has designed reward structures for campaigns that have raised $500K+ on Kickstarter and Indiegogo. Create a complete reward tier system for the following campaign.

PROJECT BRIEF:
- Product/Project Name: [NAME]
- What it does: [1-2 sentences]
- Retail price (planned): $[AMOUNT] (or "unknown" if pre-pricing)
- Platform: [Kickstarter / Indiegogo]
- Funding Goal: $[AMOUNT]
- Production cost estimate per unit: $[AMOUNT] (or "unknown")
- Extras/add-ons available: [accessories, bundles, color variants, digital extras, etc.]
- Signature perk you can offer to top backers: [factory tour, name in credits, custom engraving, 1:1 call, etc.]

Design the following reward tiers:

TIER STRUCTURE:
1. THANK YOU (no product) — $1–5
   Name it something meaningful. Give backers a digital token of appreciation (wallpaper, PDF guide, access to private update feed). Purpose: captures lapsed backers, inflates backer count for social proof.

2. EARLY BIRD (product, limited quantity) — price it 25-35% below retail
   Name it with urgency (Early Bird, Founding Backer, Launch Special). Quantity limit: 200–500 units. Deadline: campaign end date. This tier drives the first 48-hour spike.

3. BACKER SPECIAL (product, main tier) — price it 15-20% below retail
   This is the workhorse tier. Price it as the "smart" choice. Unlimited quantity.

4. DOUBLE DOWN — 2 units
   For gifters and couples. Price it as "buy 2, save more" (not just 2× single tier). Name it accordingly (Gift Bundle, Partner Pack, etc.).

5. PRO / POWER USER — product + premium add-on
   Bundle the core product with the most-requested accessory or upgrade. Price at 20-25% less than buying separately.

6. FAMILY / TEAM PACK — 3-5 units
   For households, teams, or small businesses. Price per unit should be lowest of any tier (drives bulk backing).

7. SUPERFAN — limited edition, exclusive, numbered
   Max 50 units. Significantly higher price. Includes everything from previous tiers PLUS the signature perk. Frame as "collector" or "founding member."

8. ORGANIZATION / WHOLESALE (optional, only if relevant) — 10+ units
   For businesses, schools, or organizations. Include a contact email for custom quotes.

For EACH tier, output:
- TIER NAME: [creative, product-relevant name]
- PRICE: $[amount]
- QUANTITY LIMIT: [number or "unlimited"]
- ESTIMATED DELIVERY: [Month Year]
- INCLUDES: [bulleted list of what's in the tier]
- DESCRIPTION: [2-3 sentences of copy using scarcity, value, and transformation language]
- URGENCY TRIGGER: [specific line to add to tier description to drive action]

After all tiers, add:
PRICING PSYCHOLOGY NOTES:
- Which tier is the decoy (makes the main tier look like the best deal)
- Which tier will drive the most revenue (volume × price)
- Recommended "featured" tier to highlight in the campaign editor
- Early bird sell-out strategy (when to switch from EB to main tier)
```

---

## Tips for Best Results

- If you don't know your retail price yet, estimate it based on 3-5× your production cost.
- The Early Bird tier should feel like a genuine deal, not a gimmick. Real 25%+ discount drives real urgency.
- Don't create more than 8 tiers — choice paralysis kills conversions. Less is more.
- Always include a $1 "Thank You" tier — it inflates your backer count and provides social proof.
