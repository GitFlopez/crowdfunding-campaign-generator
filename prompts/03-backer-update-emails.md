# Prompt 3: Backer Update Emails

## Purpose
Generate a 5-email sequence covering every critical campaign moment — pre-launch hype, launch-day surge, milestone celebration, shipping update, and thank-you/future offer.

---

## Prompt

```
You are an email strategist who has managed backer communications for Kickstarter campaigns that raised $250K+. Write a 5-email backer update sequence for the following campaign.

PROJECT BRIEF:
- Product/Project Name: [NAME]
- Platform: [Kickstarter / Indiegogo]
- Funding Goal: $[AMOUNT]
- Campaign Duration: [X] days
- Launch date: [DATE or "TBD"]
- Key milestone to celebrate: [e.g., "hit 50% funded in 48 hours" / "crossed 1,000 backers" / "unlocked stretch goal"]
- Estimated ship date: [Month Year]
- Any post-campaign offer: [retail launch, exclusive early access, referral discount, etc.]
- Founder first name: [NAME]
- Tone: [excited / professional / personal / heartfelt]

Write 5 emails with the following structure for each:
- EMAIL NUMBER & NAME
- SEND TIMING: [when to send this email relative to campaign]
- SUBJECT LINE: [A/B test: provide 2 options]
- PREVIEW TEXT: [40-60 chars]
- BODY: [full email, conversational, under 300 words]
- CTA: [specific action to take]
- P.S. LINE: [one post-script that adds urgency or a personal touch]

---

EMAIL 1: PRE-LAUNCH HYPE (Send: 48 hours before launch to pre-signup list)
Goal: Convert warm leads to Day 1 backers. Day 1 momentum determines algorithm placement on Kickstarter.
Include: What's launching, why now, early bird tier reminder (limited quantity), direct link to "notify me" page.
Hook: Make them feel like insiders getting advance access.

EMAIL 2: LAUNCH DAY (Send: Campaign goes live — immediately)
Goal: Drive the first 24-hour spike. Algorithms reward early velocity.
Include: We're live, here's the link, early bird tier is limited (how many left), social sharing ask (share = more backers = faster funded = better product for everyone).
Hook: "This is the moment" energy. Make them feel part of something.

EMAIL 3: MILESTONE CELEBRATION (Send: When you hit 50% funded or a major milestone)
Goal: Maintain momentum in the "dead middle" of the campaign (days 4-25 are the slowest).
Include: Celebrate the milestone with specific numbers, thank backers by name (use "you"), tease a stretch goal or upcoming surprise, ask for referrals with a simple sharing ask.
Hook: Social proof + community energy. They're part of something that's working.

EMAIL 4: FINAL 48 HOURS (Send: 48 hours before campaign closes)
Goal: Urgency-driven surge. Final 48 hours are 2nd biggest spike after Day 1.
Include: Campaign closes [DATE/TIME], early bird tier gone (if applicable), how many backers are in, what happens next (manufacturing timeline), last chance to join founding backer pricing forever.
Hook: FOMO + finality. After this, retail price. After this, no more exclusive perks.

EMAIL 5: CAMPAIGN COMPLETE + NEXT STEPS (Send: Within 24 hours of campaign close)
Goal: Lock in goodwill, reduce refund requests, set expectations, plant seed for post-campaign offer.
Include: Total raised (celebrate the number), thank you that feels personal not corporate, manufacturing timeline, next update date, post-campaign offer (if applicable — early access, referral discount, etc.).
Hook: Gratitude + clarity. They made the right choice. Here's what happens next.

Format each email so it's ready to copy-paste into Kickstarter's Update editor or a standard email client. Use the founder's first name as the sign-off.
```

---

## Tips for Best Results

- The pre-launch email (Email 1) should only go to your pre-signup list — NOT to existing backers.
- Emails 2-5 go to backers via Kickstarter/Indiegogo's built-in update system.
- Keep emails under 300 words — backers skim updates, they don't read them.
- Always include a P.S. line — it's the second-most-read part of any email after the subject line.
- For GoFundMe campaigns, skip Email 1 (no pre-signup system) and adjust Email 3 to focus on donor appreciation over milestones.
