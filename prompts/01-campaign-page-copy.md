# Prompt 1: Campaign Page Copy

## Purpose
Generate the full main campaign page copy for a Kickstarter, Indiegogo, or GoFundMe campaign — hook, story, problem/solution, product narrative, timeline, team credibility, FAQ, and call to action.

---

## Prompt

```
You are a crowdfunding copywriter who has written campaign pages for 6-figure funded projects on Kickstarter and Indiegogo. Write a complete campaign page for the following project.

PROJECT BRIEF:
- Product/Project Name: [NAME]
- What it does: [1-2 sentences]
- Who it's for: [target backer persona]
- Platform: [Kickstarter / Indiegogo / GoFundMe]
- Funding Goal: $[AMOUNT]
- Campaign Duration: [X] days
- Origin Story: [why you built this — be specific, personal if possible]
- Key Features/Benefits: [top 3-5 things that make this special]
- Team: [founder name + most relevant credential]
- Existing Traction: [prototypes, pre-signups, press coverage, patents, etc.]
- Tone: [passionate / professional / playful / urgent]

Write the campaign page with these sections:

HOOK (50-75 words)
Open with a vivid scene or statement that makes the target backer feel the pain this solves. Do NOT open with the product name or "Introducing." Start in the middle of a moment.

THE PROBLEM (100-150 words)
Amplify the problem using sensory language. Make the reader feel it, not just understand it. Show the cost of the problem (time, money, frustration, missed opportunity).

THE SOLUTION (150-200 words)
Introduce the product as the transformation, not just a tool. Lead with what the backer's life looks like AFTER using it. Then explain how it works. Use a "Here's how it works" transition.

WHY WE BUILT THIS (100-150 words)
Tell the origin story. Be specific — name the moment, the frustration, the failed alternatives. First-person, founder voice.

WHAT MAKES [PRODUCT NAME] DIFFERENT (150-200 words)
List 3-5 key differentiators as short paragraphs. Each one should contrast directly with how competitors or alternatives fail.

THE TIMELINE (50-75 words)
Manufacturing/delivery timeline in plain language. Include key milestones: prototype status, tooling, production, shipping. Be honest about risks and how you're mitigating them.

MEET THE TEAM (75-100 words)
1-3 team members. Each gets: name, role, and the ONE credential most relevant to why they can execute this.

FAQ (8 questions)
Answer the 8 most common objections that kill crowdfunding conversions:
1. Is this a real product or just a concept?
2. When will I receive my order?
3. What if the campaign doesn't reach its goal?
4. What's your return/refund policy?
5. Do you ship internationally?
6. [Product-specific technical question based on the brief]
7. [Product-specific use case question based on the brief]
8. What happens after the campaign ends?

CALL TO ACTION (50-75 words)
End with urgency (early bird pricing ends, limited units, campaign end date) and a direct instruction to back now. Include one social proof element (pre-signups, press mention, prototype testers) to reduce risk.

Format the output as clean sections with headers, ready to paste into the campaign editor. Write in second person ("you") for problem/solution sections and first person ("we") for team/origin sections.
```

---

## Tips for Best Results

- The more specific your origin story, the better the output. Vague inputs = generic copy.
- If you have press quotes or beta tester testimonials, add them in the "existing traction" field.
- Run the output through your own voice filter — add specific numbers, names, and details that only you know.
- For GoFundMe personal causes, change the tone to "heartfelt" and remove the "What Makes It Different" section.
